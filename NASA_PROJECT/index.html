Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) is an advanced remote sensing technology that uses radar signals to create high-resolution images of the Earth's surface.

SAR can "see" through clouds, fog, and darkness, providing continuous monitoring capabilities day and night.

üì° How Does SAR Work?

1. Signal Transmission

The satellite transmits pulses of microwave energy toward the Earth's surface.

2. Echo Reception

Different surfaces reflect signals differently - water, vegetation, and soil all have unique radar signatures.

3. Image Processing

Advanced algorithms process the returned signals to create detailed images.
